# Technician Cram Sheet — Rapid Fire Review

## Formulas

Ohm's Law. Voltage equals current times resistance. Current equals voltage divided by resistance. Resistance equals voltage divided by current.

Power. Watts equals volts times amps. Watts equals current squared times resistance. Watts equals voltage squared divided by resistance.

Wavelength in meters equals 300 divided by frequency in megahertz.

Half-wave dipole length in feet: 468 divided by frequency in megahertz.

Quarter-wave vertical antenna length in feet: 234 divided by frequency in megahertz.

## Decibels

Plus 3 dB: double the power. Minus 3 dB: half the power.

Plus 10 dB: ten times the power. Minus 10 dB: one tenth the power.

Plus 20 dB: one hundred times the power.

Decibels stack. 3 plus 3 equals 6 dB, which is four times the power.

## Metric Prefixes

Giga: one billion. Mega: one million. Kilo: one thousand.

Milli: one thousandth. Micro: one millionth. Pico: one trillionth.

## Key Numbers

License term: 10 years. Grace period: 2 years. Can renew during grace period but cannot transmit.

Station ID: every 10 minutes and at the end of your last transmission. ID by voice or CW.

Maximum power: 1500 watts PEP. Always use minimum power necessary.

Technician HF CW power limit: 200 watts PEP.

No minimum age for a ham license. Only the FCC grants licenses. Exams given by Volunteer Examiners.

## Band Privileges

HF bands, Technicians, limited access.

80 meters: CW only, 200 watts. 40 meters: CW only, 200 watts. 15 meters: CW only, 200 watts.

10 meters: 28.000 to 28.300 megahertz for CW, RTTY, and data. 28.300 to 28.500 megahertz for CW and SSB. 200 watts on both segments.

VHF and UHF, Technicians get full privileges, all modes, 1500 watts.

6 meters: 50 to 54 megahertz. 2 meters: 144 to 148 megahertz. 1.25 meters: 222 to 225 megahertz. 70 centimeters: 420 to 450 megahertz. 23 centimeters: 1240 to 1300 megahertz.

## Key Frequencies

2 meter calling frequency: 146.520 megahertz. 70 centimeter calling frequency: 446.000 megahertz. 6 meter calling frequency: 52.525 megahertz.

156.800 megahertz is NOT amateur. That's marine channel 16.

## Repeater Offsets

2 meters: plus or minus 600 kilohertz. 70 centimeters: plus or minus 5 megahertz.

1.25 meters: minus 1.6 megahertz. 6 meters: minus 1 megahertz or minus 500 kilohertz.

## Operating Rules

Control operator must be present or operating by remote control.

Third-party traffic: allowed only with countries that have third-party agreements.

Prohibited: music, obscenity, false signals, encryption to hide meaning.

Encryption allowed only for space station commands or radio control model craft.

Transmit without ID only when controlling model craft.

Operating another person's station: your privileges apply, not the station owner's.

## Propagation

VHF and UHF: line of sight. Limited by the horizon.

Sporadic E: surprise VHF skip from E-layer ionization patches.

Tropospheric ducting: VHF and UHF signals bend through weather inversions.

Meteor scatter: brief VHF reflections off ionized meteor trails.

F-layer skip: HF signals bounce off the F-layer. Works on 10 meters and below.

## Safety

Every station must do an RF exposure evaluation.

Keep antennas at least 10 feet from power lines.

Disconnect feed lines when station is not in use. Lightning protection.

Never climb a tower alone. Always have a ground helper.

Turn off power and lock out before working on equipment.

RF burns: caused by touching an antenna during transmission.

## Components

Resistor: opposes current flow. Measured in ohms.

Capacitor: stores energy in an electric field. Measured in farads.

Inductor: stores energy in a magnetic field. Measured in henrys.

Diode: allows current in one direction only. LED: a diode that emits light.

Transistor: amplifies or switches signals. Fuse: breaks the circuit on overcurrent.

Voltage regulator: maintains constant output voltage.

## Modulation Types

FM: frequency varies with audio. Standard for VHF and UHF voice.

AM: amplitude varies. Used on airband and some HF.

SSB: single sideband. Efficient HF voice. Upper sideband above 10 megahertz. Lower sideband below.

CW: Morse code. Most efficient mode. Narrowest bandwidth.

FT8: digital weak-signal mode. Very popular.

DMR: digital voice on FM channels using time-division multiplexing.

## Exam Traps

Wavelength formula: 300 divided by megahertz. Not times. Divided by.

Know all three Ohm's Law forms cold.

Technicians get 28.0 to 28.5 megahertz. Not all of 10 meters.

Minimum power necessary. That's the FCC general power rule.
